Questions Families Ask

Answer concerns before the first call.

A short FAQ keeps people on the page longer and helps case managers understand whether to contact you.

What type of residents may be a good fit?

Adults who need a smaller residential care setting, daily supervision, personal care support, medication assistance, meals, structure, and respectful routines may be a good fit. Final fit depends on assessment, care needs, licensing requirements, and current availability.

Do you support residents with developmental disabilities?

We welcome inquiries for adults with developmental disabilities who need daily structure, supervision, personal care support, and a home-like environment.

Do you accept dementia-related care inquiries?

We welcome dementia-related inquiries and review each situation based on care needs, safety, supervision needs, and whether the home is appropriate for the resident.

Can families or case managers schedule a visit?

Yes. Families, guardians, representatives, case managers, placement coordinators, and discharge planners can contact us to discuss fit and schedule a visit when appropriate.
